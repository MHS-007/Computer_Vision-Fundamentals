# Computer Vision Fundamentals

A comprehensive collection of notes covering the fundamentals of computer vision, from basic concepts to advanced applications and future trends.

## üìö Overview

This repository contains detailed notes on computer vision fundamentals, organized into 10 comprehensive sections. These notes cover everything from image representation and processing to deep learning applications, ethical considerations, and future trends in the field.

## üìñ Contents

### Section 01: Introduction to Computer Vision
- Overview of computer vision and its goals
- Applications across various domains (Healthcare, Automotive, Manufacturing, Retail, Entertainment)
- Three-stage process: Image Acquisition ‚Üí Image Processing ‚Üí Image Analysis

### Section 02: Image Representation and Processing
- **Digital Images and Pixels**: Resolution, bit depth, RGB color model
- **Image Types**: Binary, Grayscale, Color, Multispectral images
- **Image Formats**: JPEG, PNG, TIFF
- **Image Filtering and Enhancement**: Smoothing, sharpening, edge detection, contrast adjustment

### Section 03: Image Features and Descriptors
- **Corner Detection**: Harris, Shi-Tomasi, FAST methods
- **Feature Extraction**: SIFT, SURF, ORB
- **Image Descriptors**: HOG, LBP, CNN-based descriptors
- **Feature Matching**: Brute Force, FLANN, RANSAC

### Section 04: Image Classification and Object Recognition
- **Machine Learning in CV**: K-NN, SVM, Decision Trees, Random Forests
- **Object Detection and Localization**: Region proposal, classification, Non-Maximum Suppression
- **Evaluation Metrics**: Intersection over Union (IoU)

### Section 05: Image Segmentation and Understanding
- **Segmentation Methods**: Pixel-based, Region-based, Edge-based segmentation
- **Thresholding**: Global, Adaptive, Otsu's Method
- **Region-Based Segmentation**: Region growing, splitting and merging
- **Semantic Segmentation**: U-Net, Mask R-CNN, DeepLab

### Section 06: Motion Analysis and Tracking
- **Optical Flow**: Block matching, differential methods (Lucas-Kanade, Horn-Schunck)
- **Object Tracking**: Kalman Filter, Mean Shift, Particle Filter
- **Motion-Based Applications**: Activity recognition, gesture recognition, video stabilization
- **Surveillance and Security**: Motion detection, crowd analysis, traffic monitoring

### Section 07: 3D Computer Vision
- **Depth Perception**: Monocular, binocular, and motion cues
- **Stereoscopic Vision**: Stereo matching and rectification
- **Structure from Motion (SfM)**: 3D reconstruction from multiple viewpoints
- **3D Reconstruction Methods**: Volumetric and surface-based methods

### Section 08: Applications of Computer Vision
- **Face Recognition and Biometrics**: Detection, alignment, identification, verification
- **Surveillance and Security Systems**: Activity monitoring, anomaly detection
- **Augmented Reality (AR) and Virtual Reality (VR)**: Applications in education, entertainment, healthcare

### Section 09: Ethical Considerations in Computer Vision
- **Privacy and Data Protection**: Consent, identity, security, ownership
- **Bias and Fairness**: Data bias, algorithm bias, impact bias, accountability
- **Ethical Use**: Guidelines for responsible development and deployment

### Section 10: Future Trends in Computer Vision
- **Deep Learning Advancements**: Self-supervised learning, GANs, Explainable AI
- **Robotics and Autonomous Systems**: Autonomous vehicles, drones, humanoid robots
- **Emerging Applications**: Social good, art and creativity, personalization
